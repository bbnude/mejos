About

Seafood refers to any form related to sea life that is regarded as food by humans. It comprises edible aquatic animals but excludes mammals. In simple words, it includes both ocean creatures and freshwater creatures. It is significantly comprised of shellfish and fish. Strong evidence has shown that eating fish and other seafoods improve brain, eye, and heart health. The new 2020-2025 Dietary Guidelines for Americans (DGA) recommend that Americans of all ages should eat more seafood-at least twice a week-particularly pregnant women and young children.

Sea urchins, spiny marine creatures belonging to the phylum Echinodermata, are known for their delicate and flavorful roe, which is a prized delicacy in many culinary traditions worldwide. The tips of the sea urchin spines have tiny claw-like pincers called pedicellariae which carry venom. Symptoms vary depending on the species.

Kinilaw (pronounced [kɪnɪˈlaʊ] or [kɪˈnɪlaʊ], literally "eaten raw") is a raw seafood dish and preparation method native to the Philippines. It is sometimes also referred to as Philippine ceviche due to its similarity to the Latin American dish ceviche. But kinilaw, a lightly sweet, tart, and tangy raw seafood appetizer prepared in every corner of the country, comes very close to epitomizing the Filipino palate.

My Favorites

Swaki

One of my favorite seafoods of all time is Swaki or sea urchin, it has a sweet, salty, and umami flavor, and it is very easy to catch. My family has been harvesting Swaki since I was a kid, and this is the first on our table when we have a family bonding.

Kinason

This is the reason why, go to seashore every low-tibe. This are called Kinason or shellfish, it has a little sweet, a little salty, and a little minerally. Their flavor reflects the water in which they grow.

Kinilaw

Kinilaw is very common in Philippine especially to those people who lives in the sea-side. Me and my friends often catch small fish in the shore to make a Kinilaw. This is thing we do when my friends visits me.
